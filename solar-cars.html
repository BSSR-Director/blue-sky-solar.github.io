Solar Cars
Our present car, Borealis, and our past cars: Viridian, Polaris, and Horizon!
GEN 11: BOREALIS
In the current cycle, our team is working rigorously and diligently on refining our Gen 11 solar car Borealis. Building upon the solar cars that came before us, and innovating upon strategies from our over 20-year long history, Borealis is raring to go at the World Solar Challenge held in October 2023 in the Australian outback.
GEN X: Viridian
Loaded with our first monocoque chassis and aluminum suspension system, Viridian was built on a drastic overhaul in design flexibility and strength-to-weight ratio. With a custom battery management system, our first fully software-controlled electrical system boosts both accessible maintenance and reduced system weight. Furthermore, in pursuit of fabricating our most aerodynamic vehicle yet, Viridian's top aerobody is doubly-curved, a challenging but rewarding feat of engineering.

GEN 8: Horizon
Horizon is the team's first catamaran where the driver compartment is located on one side to maximize array efficiency. The team learnt from experience that every time in WSC, there would be one day with extreme weather conditions. It was not different for Horizon - a dust storm hit the team half way through the challenge. Luckily, it was not during the racing hours. Horizon was also a long-awaited return to the American Solar Challenge, since 2003 when Faust II placed 11th back then.

Competing in the 2015 World Solar Challenge, in the Challenger Class, Horizon placed 11th place overall. In addition, Horizon marked Blue Sky Solar Racing's return to the American Solar Challenge in 2016 after a 13 year absence, where it placed 3rd place overall.
